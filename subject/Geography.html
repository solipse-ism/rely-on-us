  • Coasts
    • Wave Action
      • Destructive wave - Erosion
        • Backswash > Swash
        • High frequency
        • High amplitude
        • elliptical
      • Constructive wave - Deposition
        • Everything is opposite to destructive waves
    • Transportation
      • Traction - rock drag along sea floor
      • Saltation - rock bounce along sea floor
      • Suspension - rock carried along with water
      • Solution - dissolve sediment
      • Longshore Drift - due to the slightly angled direction of prevailing wind and backwash down by gravity.
      • Mass Movement - removal of cliff-face material due to gravity
        • Landslide - soil and rock move together until it hit bottom of the slope. sloped cliff
        • Slumping - weak rock fall due to curve surface beneath it, wet soil
        • Rockfall - fragment of rock break and fall away from cliff face . steep cliff
    • Erosion Processes - wearing away of rocks by wind and rain
      • Hydraulic action - force of wave force air into the crack inside the cliff
      • Abrasion - carried rock hits the cliff
      • Corrasion - Rocks are flung at cliff by wave
      • Attrition - Rock hit each other and become smoother
      • Solution - Cliff's rock dissolve into water, acidic
    • Deposition
      • decrease in energy or velocity of wave or wind
      • meterial is deposited
    • Weathering - breakdown of rock in situ
      • Mechanical - Freeze-thaw
        • water get into crack in rock
        • freeze, which make the crack wider
        • overtime rock becomes cracked and seperated
      • Chemical - Acid Rain
        • Acid react with mineral in rock
        • e.g. limestone
        • react faster in hotter environment
      • Biological - Growth of plant root
        • Root grows into crack
        • it gets bigger and makes the crack wider
        • rock split apart
    • Geology (rock type)
      • Hard rock - limestone (rugged landscape)
      • Soft rock - sand, gravel (flat landscape)
      • Structure
      • Concordant - alternating hard and soft rock parallel to oncoming wave
      • Discordant - alternating hard and soft rock perpendicular to oncoming wave
      • Bay form when soft rock eroded, discordant. Cove forms when bits of hard rock then soft rock inside is eroded, concordant

      • Stack

        • arch collapse

      • Stump
        • erosion on stack
        • becomes stack

        • become notch
      • Wave-cut platform

        • cliff collapse and retreat
        • the uneroded base become platform

    • Tropical Cyclone
      • Saffir-Simpson Scale, cat 1-5
      • landfall - when cyclone enter the land
      • Requirement
        • warm ocean - 27C
        • low wind shear
        • Coriolis force
        • ocean depth 70m or more
      • Formation
        • warm water evaporate
        • warm moist air rises upward, creating strong wind
        • water vapour cools and condense into cumulonimbus cloud and spin due to Coriolis effect(which is due to Earth rotation)
        • Smaller storms join together to make bigger storm and increase wind speed
        • prevailing wind push storm toward land
        • storm slow down as it hit land due to friction (landfall)
      • Hazard
        • Strong wind - destroy tree, crop, building, power supply and communication
        • torrential rain - can lead to flooding, trigger landslide/mudslide
        • storm surges - (rise in sea level due to wind pushing them onto land) destroy crop due to (salty water), flood
    • Tectonic Plate Boundary
      • movement of plate cause by movement of mantle due to convection current
      • Destructive - 2 plates move into each other
      • Constructive - 2 plates move away from each other
      • Conservative - 2 plates move parallel either opposite direction or same direction at different speed, released friction between rough rock cause EQ
      • Collision - 2 continental plate collide, and both equally dense -> fold mountain formed
    • Volcano
      • VEI scale, volcano explosive index, 0-8
      • Hotspot - an area on earth crust where there are alot of plumes of magma escaping through crust
        • crust thinner, it rise through
        • convectional current makes it rise
        • hawaii
        • sheild volcano
      • Strato - narrow base and steep side, explosive eruption, andesitic magama, acidic and viscous lava (mostly found in destructive plate boundary)
        • More dense oceanic crust subduct as below continental plate/oceanic plate into mantle
        • Friction can cause Earthquake between the 2 plates along the Benioff zone
        • The sub-ducting crust melts and form new less dense magma
        • the new magma rises up through continental/oceanic crust
      • Shield - wide base and shallow slope, effusive eruption, basaltic magma (mostly found in hotspot, constructive plate bouondary)
        • techtonic plate diverge in the constructive plate boundary
        • The moving plate is caused by convection currents in the mantle
        • This creates a mid ocean ridge
        • the rift zone is the gap in the middle
        • magma rises as basalt lava(magma on surface, pillow lava), it cools and forms new crust made out of basalt rock
